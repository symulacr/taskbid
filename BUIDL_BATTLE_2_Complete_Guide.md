## Overview & Introduction

**BUIDL BATTLE #2: The Bitcoin Builders Tournament** is a virtual hackathon hosted on DoraHacks, organized by **Stacks Labs**, and aimed at recognizing, celebrating, and propelling innovative projects built on Bitcoin and the Stacks ecosystem. With a total prize pool of **$20,000 USD**, this is one of the most exciting and rewarding Bitcoin builder events of 2026.

More than just a competition, BUIDL BATTLE is described by the organizers as *"a celebration and a launching point for the next wave of Bitcoin builders."* It is designed to give both established teams and newcomers a platform to showcase their work, gain visibility with investors and accelerators, and deepen their roots in the rapidly growing Bitcoin and Stacks developer ecosystem.

The event is fully virtual, meaning developers and builders from anywhere in the world can participate. Whether you are a seasoned Stacks developer or someone entirely new to Bitcoin-layer development, BUIDL BATTLE #2 is structured to welcome you and give your work the spotlight it deserves.

The event runs for approximately three weeks, with project submissions open for an extended window before final judging concludes and winners are announced on **March 31st, 2026**.

## What Is Stacks?

To fully appreciate the scope and intent of BUIDL BATTLE #2, it helps to understand the Stacks ecosystem that sits at its core.

**Stacks** is a Bitcoin Layer 2 (L2) protocol that enables smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps) to be built on top of Bitcoin — the world's most secure, decentralized, and widely recognized blockchain. Bitcoin itself is notoriously limited in programmability by design, prioritizing security and simplicity. Stacks bridges this gap by introducing a full smart contract environment while anchoring its state and security to the Bitcoin base layer.

Key technologies and features within the Stacks ecosystem include:

- **Clarity** — Stacks' native smart contract programming language. Unlike Solidity (Ethereum's language), Clarity is a decidable language, meaning developers can know exactly what a contract will do before deploying it. This greatly reduces the risk of on-chain bugs and exploits.
- **sBTC** — A trust-minimized, 1:1 Bitcoin-backed asset on the Stacks chain. sBTC allows Bitcoin holders to move their BTC into the Stacks ecosystem to participate in DeFi and other applications, without needing to use centralized custodians.
- **Proof of Transfer (PoX)** — Stacks' consensus mechanism. Miners commit Bitcoin to participate in the network and, in return, earn STX tokens. This creates a unique economic link between Stacks and Bitcoin.
- **stacks.js** — A JavaScript library for building front-end applications that interact with the Stacks blockchain.
- **USDCx** — An implementation of USDC (Circle's stablecoin) on the Stacks chain, powered by Circle's xReserve protocol, which enables cross-chain usage of USDC via a programmatic bridge.
- **Bitflow** — A decentralized exchange (DEX) within the Stacks ecosystem that can be used as a DeFi building block.
- **x402 Protocol** — A payment protocol designed to power agentic commerce — enabling automated agents (or "molbots") to send and receive payments programmatically on Stacks.

BUIDL BATTLE #2 encourages participants to leverage as many of these technologies as possible in their project, as **Stacks alignment is one of the official judging criteria**.

## Competition Structure

BUIDL BATTLE #2 is divided into **two main components**:

### 1. The Main Hackathon

The main hackathon is **open submission**, meaning participants are free to build whatever they want — as long as the project uses Stacks or technologies from the Stacks ecosystem. There are no mandatory themes for the main category. Your imagination and creativity are the only limits.

The main hackathon features:
- **One single round of judging** — there are no elimination rounds or multiple phases. All projects are reviewed once by the full panel.
- A **panel of judges** with diverse expertise, drawn from the Stacks Labs team, investors, grant funds, and accelerator leads.
- **Three placement prizes** for first, second, and third place.

This open format is designed to encourage maximum creativity. Whether you want to build a DeFi protocol, an NFT marketplace, a developer tool, a DAO governance system, or something entirely novel, the main hackathon is your stage.

### 2. Bounties

In addition to the main hackathon, **three dedicated bounties** are available, each worth **$3,000 USD**. These bounties are awarded independently of the main hackathon placements, meaning a single project can qualify for both a placement prize and one or more bounties simultaneously.

Each bounty has a dedicated expert judge from the sponsoring project or protocol who selects the single best submission for their bounty theme. Bounties are thematic and more specific than the open main hackathon — details for each bounty are provided in the [Bounty Challenges](#bounty-challenges) section below.

This dual-track structure means that if your project is highly innovative but also happens to use a specific technology deeply (like sBTC or USDCx), you could realistically earn both a placement prize and a bounty reward from the same submission.

## Submission Requirements

All participants must meet the following requirements to have their project considered in BUIDL BATTLE #2:

1. **Submit via DoraHacks** — Your project must be submitted through the DoraHacks platform and listed in the BUIDL Gallery. Submitting your work makes it visible to organizers, judges, and the broader community.

2. **GitHub Repository** — Every submission must include a publicly accessible link to your project's source code hosted on GitHub, GitLab, or Bitbucket. A link is required — no exceptions.

3. **Working Demo** — Judges need to see your project in action. This means a live demo, a hosted deployment, or at minimum a recorded video walkthrough that demonstrates the functionality of your application.

4. **Pitch Video (Under 5 Minutes)** — A short video pitch summarizing what your project is, why it matters, how it works, and how it uses Stacks. This is a critical component of your submission — it gives judges a clear, digestible view of your work and should highlight your project's unique value proposition.

Meeting all four criteria is non-negotiable. A submission missing any of these elements may be disqualified or reviewed less favorably. Notably, once submitted, projects go through a review process before they are considered officially accepted. Based on community feedback, this review process can take several hours and is conducted by the Stacks Labs team.

## Bounty Challenges

### 1. Best Use of USDCx — $3,000 USDCX

**Bounty Sponsor:** Circle

**About Circle & USDCx:**
Circle is the issuer of USDC, one of the world's most widely used stablecoins. The **xReserve protocol** is Circle's infrastructure for enabling cross-chain usage of USDC, and **USDCx** is the specific implementation of this on the Stacks blockchain. USDCx allows developers on Stacks to work with a stable, dollar-pegged asset and to leverage Circle's programmatic bridge for moving value across chains.

**What the Judges Are Looking For:**
This bounty is awarded to the project that makes the **best use of USDCx on Stacks**. The use case can involve the token on its own — for example, as a payment rail, a savings mechanism, or a DeFi liquidity asset — or it can involve an innovative use of the **programmatic bridge** that powers USDCx.

The key word here is "best" — judges from Circle will be looking for projects that use USDCx in a meaningful, creative, and impactful way, not just projects that happen to include USDCx as a minor feature.

**Important Note for Builders:**
As of the time of this document, testnet USDCx is not officially documented in the hackathon's provided materials, and the main bridge at `https://bridge.stacks.co/usdc/eth/stx` is a mainnet tool. Participants who want to test USDCx integrations are encouraged to reach out to the Stacks organizers or community directly via Skool or Discord to inquire about testnet access or faucet availability.

### 2. Most Innovative Use of sBTC — $3,000 USDCX

**Bounty Sponsor:** Stacks

**About sBTC:**
**sBTC** is one of the most significant and exciting developments in the Stacks ecosystem. It is a **trust-minimized, 1:1 Bitcoin-backed asset** native to the Stacks chain. What makes sBTC special is that it allows Bitcoin holders to bring their BTC into the Stacks ecosystem and use it in DeFi, dApps, and smart contracts, while remaining anchored to the security of the Bitcoin network.

sBTC removes the need for centralized custodians or wrapped token solutions that rely on trusted third parties, making it a fundamental building block for a truly decentralized Bitcoin economy. It represents Stacks' core mission: unlocking the economic potential of Bitcoin for the world.

**What the Judges Are Looking For:**
This bounty is explicitly focused on **innovation**. Judges want to see the most creative, novel, and impactful application of sBTC. There are no thematic restrictions — the project could be:

- A DeFi protocol (lending, borrowing, liquidity provision) powered by sBTC
- A privacy-preserving application using sBTC
- A user experience (UX) improvement that makes sBTC more accessible
- A developer tool or infrastructure layer built around sBTC
- Anything else that uses sBTC in a new and compelling way

The emphasis is on doing something **genuinely new** with sBTC — pushing the boundaries of what is possible with a programmable, trust-minimized Bitcoin asset.

### 3. Best x402 Integration — $3,000 USDCX

**Bounty Sponsor:** x402 Stacks

**About x402:**
**x402** is a payment protocol built for **agentic commerce** — that is, automated payments made by and between software agents ("molbots") without human intervention. The x402 implementation for Stacks is built specifically to work within the Stacks ecosystem, using sBTC and USDCx as payment assets.

The vision of x402 is a future where autonomous AI agents can delegate tasks to one another and compensate each other for services, all on-chain and without centralized intermediaries. This represents a convergence of AI, Web3, and Bitcoin — arguably one of the most forward-looking technical frontiers in the entire crypto space.

**What the Judges Are Looking For:**
The x402 bounty wants participants to think big and build the **future of molbot-to-molbot commerce**. Specific ideas the sponsor has suggested include:

- A protocol where molbots can pay each other using **sBTC + x402**
- A molbot that can shop and receive payment using x402
- Specialized "skill molbots" (e.g., high-quality content generators) that **charge for their services using USDCx + x402**
- Any task a molbot could perform and be compensated for using x402 on Stacks

This bounty is the most open-ended and experimental of the three, rewarding participants who are willing to explore uncharted territory and build something truly futuristic.

## Judging Criteria

All main hackathon submissions will be evaluated by a panel of judges across five core dimensions. Understanding these criteria is essential for crafting a competitive submission.

### 1. Innovation (⭐ High Importance)
How unique is your approach? Does your project solve an existing problem in a new way, or does it open up an entirely new category of possibility? Judges will reward creative problem-solving and original ideas over incremental improvements to existing solutions.

### 2. Technical Implementation (⭐ High Importance)
The quality and depth of your code matters. Judges will look at code quality, security considerations (especially important in DeFi and smart contract contexts), and the overall technical sophistication of your implementation. A robust, secure, well-structured codebase will score significantly higher than a rushed, fragile prototype.

### 3. Stacks Alignment (⭐ Core Criterion)
This criterion is unique to BUIDL BATTLE and reflects the mission of the competition. Judges will assess how well your solution **leverages Stacks' unique properties** and components of the Stacks technology stack. This includes:
- Use of **Clarity** for smart contracts
- Integration of **sBTC**
- Proof of Transfer (PoX) mechanisms
- Use of **stacks.js** or other developer tooling
- Integration with ecosystem protocols like **Bitflow**, **USDCx**, etc.

Projects that deeply and meaningfully use Stacks technology will score significantly higher than projects that only superficially interact with the ecosystem.

### 4. User Experience (⭐ Medium-High Importance)
Your project should be accessible to both **crypto-native users** and **mainstream users** unfamiliar with blockchain technology. Clean UI/UX design, clear onboarding, and ease of use will positively impact your score. The judges understand that Bitcoin DeFi can be intimidating — projects that lower the barrier to entry will stand out.

### 5. Impact Potential (⭐ Strategic Importance)
What is the projected long-term contribution of your project to the Stacks ecosystem? Judges will think about scalability, adoption potential, and strategic alignment with Stacks' mission to unlock the Bitcoin economy. If your project could grow into a major protocol or widely-used tool, make sure your pitch communicates that vision clearly.

## Community Q&A — Common Questions Answered

### Q: How do I get testnet USDCx for testing?
The main bridge at `https://bridge.stacks.co/usdc/eth/stx` is a **mainnet** tool and should not be used for testing. Testnet USDCx is not formally documented in the current hackathon materials. The recommended path is to contact the organizers directly through the **Skool group** or **Discord** to ask about testnet faucet access or alternative testing methods.

### Q: What type of project must be delivered?
Your project can be anything as long as it uses Stacks or technologies from the Stacks ecosystem. You have complete creative freedom in the main hackathon. The only non-negotiables are the submission requirements: a GitHub repo, a working demo, and a pitch video under 5 minutes.

### Q: My submission has been "under review" for over 13 hours — is there a problem?
No — this is normal. All submissions go through a review process by Stacks Labs before being officially accepted. Extended review periods are expected and do not indicate a problem with your submission. As long as you have included all required materials (GitHub, demo, video), your submission should be fine. If you are concerned, reach out to organizers through the Skool group for direct confirmation.

### Q: Can I win both a main hackathon prize and a bounty?
**Yes.** Bounties are judged independently from the main hackathon. A project that wins first place in the main hackathon is still fully eligible to win one, two, or all three bounties simultaneously. There is no restriction on winning multiple prizes.

### Q: Who judges the bounties?
Each bounty has a **dedicated expert judge** from the sponsoring project or protocol. The sBTC bounty is judged by a Stacks expert, the USDCx bounty by a Circle representative, and the x402 bounty by an x402 Stacks representative. These are domain specialists with deep knowledge of the specific technologies — so a superficial integration is unlikely to impress them.

## Tips for a Winning Submission

Based on the judging criteria and bounty descriptions, here are strategic recommendations for maximizing your chances of winning:

1. **Go deep on Stacks alignment.** Don't just build something that happens to touch the Stacks blockchain. Build something that is fundamentally enabled by Stacks' unique properties — sBTC, Clarity's decidability, or PoX mechanics. Judges will notice the difference.

2. **Start with a clear problem statement.** The most compelling pitch videos and submissions begin by clearly articulating the problem being solved. Innovation for its own sake is less compelling than innovation that solves a real pain point.

3. **Prioritize your pitch video.** With a single round of judging and a large pool of submissions (78 BUIDLs were submitted as of deadline), your pitch video is often the first and most influential impression judges will have. Make it clear, concise, and compelling. Under 5 minutes means you need to be ruthless about what you include.

4. **Target at least one bounty intentionally.** If you are building with USDCx, sBTC, or x402, make sure your submission is also explicitly entered into the corresponding bounty. The additional $3,000 per bounty significantly raises your total earning potential at no extra cost.

5. **Consider UX carefully.** The judging criteria explicitly mention accessibility for mainstream users. A clean, intuitive interface can differentiate your project from technically superior but hard-to-use alternatives.

6. **Document your code well.** Judges reviewing 78+ submissions will appreciate a well-documented GitHub repository with a clear README, setup instructions, and architecture overview.

7. **Think about post-hackathon potential.** The bonus ecosystem support is awarded to teams that show the most potential for long-term impact. Even if you don't win first place monetarily, demonstrating a clear roadmap and growth vision in your pitch can unlock the ecosystem introduction and 1:1 session with Stacks Labs.
